General Info:

  1. All participants must comply with the eligibility criteria and submission process.

  2. The participant may submit their information in no more than one category per application. In order to participate in more than one category, the participants are required to submit a separate application for each category.

  3. A participant can apply for the GCP Awards until he/she attains the age of 15; even if the participant has won an award in previous entries, he/she can still apply; there is no bar on the same.

  4. Only the names of registered or nominated participants of the Global Child Prodigy Award will be considered for the final list or winner list of the GCP Awards.

  5. The GCPA officials have the right to cancel and annul an award if the recipient commits acts of criminal misbehavior or otherwise abuses the award.

  6. Any false information or misrepresentation may lead to disqualification.

  7. Participants must adhere to the Global Child Prodigy Awards terms and conditions and code of conduct, which promote integrity, respect, and fairness.

  8. The decisions of the Global Child Prodigy Awards committee are final and binding.

  9. Winners will be announced on the official website, social media channels, and through press releases to local and international media outlets.

ELIGIBILITY AND RULES

To be eligible for the Global Child Prodigy Awards, a participant must:

  1. Age Requirement: Be under the age of 15 years at the time of the application.

  1. Area of Talent: Show talent and passion in any field, such as Acting & Theatre, Art, Cooking, Dance, Education, Entrainment, Entrepreneurship, Fashion, Gaming, Innovation & Technology, Intelligence & Memory - IQ, Language, Magician, Mentoring & Teaching, Music & Singing, Photography, Public Speaker & Presenter, Social & Environment, Space Science & Astronomy, Sports, Writing & Poetry, Yoga & Fitness, Social Media Influencer, and others.

  1. Involvement: Possess a minimum of 3 months of experience or participation in the chosen talent area, reflecting dedication and commitment to their craft. For example, if a nominee is applying for the art category, they must have been involved in the art domain for at least 3 months.

  1. Global Citizen: Be a citizen of a country that has been recognized by the United Nations.

  1. Ethics and Impact: Exhibit strong ethics and a commitment to positively impacting their community or society, and illustrate how their talent contributes to the greater good.

  2. Supporting Evidence : Participants may submit optional supporting evidence of their work, awards, or recognition on a national or international level, including but not limited to images, videos, Resume/CV, profile, certificates, honors, media coverage, or letters of recommendation.

  3. Language Proficiency: Any language is acceptable for communication during selection and subsequent events.

  4. Availability: Ideally, Awardees should be available to attend the awards ceremony, related events, and media appearances, either in person or virtually. Exceptions may be made for circumstances such as exams, sports events, health-related issues, family emergencies, or other significant commitments.


SUBMISSION PROCESS

  1. Participation: Parents, guardians, teachers, mentors, or community leaders may submit a participation entry for a child prodigy by filling out the online application form available on our website. Self-participation is also accepted for children aged 13 and above.

  2. Optional Documents: To participate, applicants may choose to submit a detailed profile, CV, or resume, along with supporting materials showcasing their talent. These may include links to videos, images of their work, links to their projects (if any), and links to their social media profiles, only if available Kindly Dont create social media if unavailable. Additionally, applicants can provide any certificates details, award details, media coverage, letters of recommendation, and all other supporting documents based on availability.

    Submitting these documents is optional, but it can help the Global Child Prodigy Awards committee better understand the applicant's work and potential. Applicants can create a Google Drive folder to store their supporting materials and share the folder link in their profile or resume. Here's a condensed 5-step guide to set up the folder:

    1. Sign in to your Google account, go to Google Drive (drive.google.com), click "New," then select "Folder" and name it (e.g., "Global Child Prodigy Awards Application").

    2. Right-click the created folder, choose "Share," and in the "Share with people and groups" window, click "Change" under "Get link." Select "Anyone with the link" in the dropdown menu and click "Done." The folder is now accessible to anyone with the link.

    3. Select "Anyone with the link" in the dropdown menu and click "Done." The folder is now accessible to anyone with the link.

    4. Add your supporting materials (e.g., profile, CV, videos, images, certificates) to the folder by dragging and dropping files into it or clicking "New" and choosing the appropriate upload option.

    5. Copy the link by clicking the "Copy link" button in the "Share with people and groups" window, and paste it into your profile or resume when submitting your application.

    Following these steps ensures that the Global Child Prodigy Awards committee can access and review your documents and materials in the shared Google Drive folder.

    Once the online application has been submitted, participants can update or add more details, works, videos, photos, and other documents by navigating to "My Profile" on the website and including the necessary information. You can keep adding information to the online portal till the GCPA Application deadline is over. Providing comprehensive and relevant information can enhance the applicant's chances of being recognized and selected as a finalist.

  3. *Deadline: The participation period will be announced on the official website and social media channels. Late submissions will not be considered.


JUDGING

  1. The Global Child Prodigy Awards Selection Committee is entrusted with the task of appraising participants' entries based on their talent, skill, potential, commitment, and innovation, without considering factors such as resources, background, popularity, or prior accomplishments. Each entry will be judged within its specific category of the online application made by the participant.

  2. The Selection Committee will supervise all aspects of the selection process, including the review and evaluation of submitted online entries. Each entry will be checked for eligibility and appropriate categorization upon submission.

  3. The information, documents, and profile provided by participants in their entries will be assessed by the committee. After a comprehensive examination of the submitted materials, the committee will identify the awardees and notify participants of their selection status within a predetermined timeframe. The results will be shared with the participants in each category.

  4. The Global Child Prodigy Awards will celebrate the winners in each category during the Awards Ceremony, scheduled for a pre-established date.
